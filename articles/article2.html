University Life: From Choosing Your Path to Graduation Success

Published on: July 20, 2025

Introduction

University can be one of the most transformative periods in your life—academically, professionally, and personally. In this post, I want to share what I learned from my own journey, from choosing a degree to walking across the graduation stage. I hope my experience can help you make the most of your own path.

Choosing the Right Program

The first step is to choose what you want to study. This means you have to find the right program at the right university that matches your career goals and your passions. During this phase, it's really important to find the right balance between what you truly like and what the job market has to offer.

If you're unsure and stuck between options—welcome to the club! I had to choose between five different programs. What helped me was creating a list of key factors I cared about, like:

  • Employability
  • Happiness
  • Personal interest
  • Location
  • Cost

Then, I rated each program from 1 to 10 based on these criteria. That gave me a clear picture of the strengths and weaknesses of each option, and made the decision more logical (and less overwhelming).

Living the University Life

Now, here it is—the big deal: university life. This is the period of your life where you have total control over what you do. You can choose to stay home and study hard for top grades, or go out every night and study in between social events—it all depends on you.

Personally, even though I succeeded academically, I strongly recommend not letting grades control your mood. It feels great to get high marks, of course, but getting a lower one is completely normal. Sometimes it's just the professor's teaching style or your own interest in the subject—and that's okay.

Time Management and Building Relationships

One of the most valuable skills I developed was time management. Creating a weekly schedule that included not just study time, but also social activities, exercise, and rest helped me stay balanced and motivated.

Equally important was building relationships. Don't underestimate the power of getting to know your professors and classmates. Participating in class, attending office hours, and forming study groups made my learning more effective—and opened doors I never expected. Some of my strongest recommendation letters came from professors who knew me beyond just my exam scores.

My Personal Approach to University

Based on my experience, I highly recommend consistently attending lectures—and staying engaged. It may sound obvious, but truly paying attention in class makes a huge difference.

And here's something many students overlook: don't be afraid to ask questions. On the contrary, ask as many as you need. Let your professors get to know you—they're there for you, not against you. Being visible and engaged enhances both your learning and your academic opportunities.

It's also completely normal to struggle sometimes. What matters most is not isolating yourself. Talk to others, share your doubts, and stay open to meeting new people. Whether it's classmates, professors, or recruiters, growing your network is always a plus.

As for studying, there's no one-size-fits-all method. Everyone needs to discover their own. Mine was simple but effective:

  • I focused intently during lectures.
  • I took just a few, very targeted notes.
  • As soon as I got home, I reviewed them quickly.
  • Before each exam, I created a single file that summarized everything I knew about the subject.

That file became my main study resource. It contained everything I needed, so I never had to waste time jumping between textbooks, lecture slides, or scattered notes. It kept things clear and efficient, and gave me confidence when exam time came.

Final Thoughts

Finally, I achieved my goal—and I can now proudly say I graduated yesterday with the highest marks and honors!

Looking back, university taught me so much more than just technical knowledge. I learned how to manage my time, how to be resilient, and how to balance ambition with well-being.

As I now prepare to start my master's journey at EPFL and ETH Zurich, I feel deeply grateful for every step—both the triumphs and the struggles.

If you're starting or currently navigating your university journey, I hope this post helped you in some way. Just remember: there's no single "right" way to do university. Trust yourself, stay curious, and embrace the ride.
